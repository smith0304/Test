Description: This book is the first full compilation of Ata Bozaci's work in different media. His graffiti pseudonym Toast is well know in the circle; other may recognize work published under his company's label, Atalier. Ata's collections range from small, intimate drawing to fantastic, larger-than-life murals. This collection captures the common threads as well as differences within this opus. The book's title is a reference to the artist's celebration of the most basic yet vital too, black ink. At the centre of this volume- both literally and figuratively- is a collection of sketches. These are the first steps in the artistic process and hence generally not shown to the public. A pity, in our opinion.
At the crux of Black Ink is the belief that a work's essence is best captured in a simple rendition: an idea does not need colour. At the start is a single black line on a blank sheet of paper. Any creative endeavour lies in the tension between the extremes of black and white. The absence of colour in this book nudges the reader towards visualizing the essentials of the picture, and is an accentuation of the idea behind it.
The book is organized into three sections: graffiti, sketches and illustrations. The first on graffiti is dedicated to Ata's work as Toast that was, in part, created under other pseudonyms. Styles and characters are followed by letter architectures and living letters. The sketches in the middle of the section of the book are divided into humans, freaks and animals. The last part of the book is devoted to illustrations. It contains work that has appeared under the Atalier label, as well as portraits.
