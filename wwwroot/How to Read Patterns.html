Description:How to Read Pattern is a practical introduction to looking at and appreciating the decorative art of pattern in textile design. It is a lavishly illustrated guide to the use of pattern, exploring themes and motifs across a range of cultural aesthetics. Small enough to carry in your pocket and serious enough to offer real answers, this comprehensive guide:

Explores the characteristics and motifs of pattern in textiles from ancient times through to contemporary designs
Provides an expert introduction to understanding pattern, followed by a thematic approach to identifying motifs and styles
Presents an accessible interpretative text and includes over 500 beautiful images from V&A Images, London.

About Author:
Clive Edwards studied the History of Design at the Royal College of Art and the Victoria & Albert Museum, and is a Reader in the History of Art and Design at Loughborough University. His published works include monographs on furnishing textiles and many contributions to journals on furniture, interiors, architecture and home furnishings. His books include the Encyclopedia of Furnishing Textiles, Soft Furnishings and Floor Coverings (2007).
